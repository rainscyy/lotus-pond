new willow · withered lotus
paintings by wu guanzhong · 1919–2010
Willow (liǔ) puns on "stay" (liú) — a Chinese motif of farewell since the Book of Songs. Withered lotus echoes Li Shangyin's late-Tang line, "leave the withered lotus to listen to the rain" — summer's quiet exit. Held in one pond — bloom and remnant breathing the same season.
